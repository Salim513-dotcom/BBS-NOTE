Advantages

  • Low cost, Speed, Flexibility, Profitable, Suitability, Collateral

Disadvantages

Trade credit (1/10, net 30)

\[APC = \frac{DR}{1-DR} \times \frac{360}{\text{Credit period} - \text{Discount period}}\] \[EAR = \left(1 + \frac{APC}{m}\right)^m - 1\]

Master rule: Credit period ∝ 1/cost, Discount period ∝ cost.

Take loan and take discountDon't take loan and don't take discount
if cost of discount > cost of loanif cost of discount < cost of loan
Take loan and don't give discountDon't take loan and give discount
if cost of discount > cost of loanif cost of discount < cost of loan

Master rule (funds generated): Take loan when cheaper → take discounts on payables, don't give discounts on receivables.

Commercial paper

Interest (FV × rate × days/360) + Transaction cost – Interest earned from reinvestment + Transaction cost on reinvestment = Cost of CP
\[APC = \frac{\text{Face value} - \text{Price}}{\text{Price}} \times \frac{360}{\text{issued period}}\]

Bank loans

Simple interest: \(APC = \frac{\text{Interest}}{\text{Loan} - \text{CB}}\), \(EAR = (1+\frac{\text{Interest}}{\text{Loan} - \text{CB}})^m -1\)
Discounted interest: \(APC = \frac{\text{Interest}}{\text{Loan} - \text{CB} - \text{Interest}}\)
Add‑on loan: Approx APC = Interest / Average loan. Actual: find installment = (Loan+Interest)/n, then solve Loan = Install × PVIFA(k%,n). ANR = k × m, EAR = \((1+ANR/m)^m -1\).

Line of credit

Cost = Interest on used amount + Commitment fee on unused amount.

Factoring of receivables

Cost = Interest + Factoring fee – Saving in credit expenses – Saving in bad debt fees.
Net amount received = Avg receivables – 20% reserve – 0.25% commission – 1% interest.
\[APC = \frac{\text{Cost of financing}}{\text{Net amount received}}\]

Chapter 3 · Term loan and lease financing

Loan payment schedule

YearOBInterestPaymentCB
End payment: Payment = Loan / PVIFA(k%,n)
Beginning payment: Payment = Loan / [PVIFA(k%,n-1)+1]

Lease vs buy

Lease: Yearly rent – tax saving → CFAT discounted.
Buy: Loan payment schedule, interest, depreciation tax saving, salvage value → CFAT discounted.

Depreciation methods

MACRSStraight lineReducing balance
Dep rate × Investment(Inv – SV)/nInvestment × %

Tax on SV = (SV – NBV) × tax rate (if any). Last year depreciation = NBV – SV.

Characteristics of term loans

  • Principal, fixed maturity, interest rate, direct negotiations, collateral, repayment schedule, restrictive covenants.

Lease vs buy factors & merits

  • Required rate, maintenance, salvage value, obsolescence, tax rate.
  • Merits: tax saving, avoid initial investment, less restrictive, 100% financing, transfer risk, liquidity, quick, low maintenance.

Demerits: more expensive, no salvage value, no depreciation tax benefit, loss of investment tax credit, difficulty in improvements.

Forms of lease

  • Operating lease, finance lease, sale and leaseback, direct lease, leveraged lease.

Convertibles (summary)

CR = Face value / CP ; CP = Face value / CR ; Initial conversion premium = (CP – \(P_0\))/\(P_0\)

AFN & financial forecasting

Required increase in assets = \(\frac{A^*}{S_0} \times \Delta S\)
Spontaneous financing = \(\frac{L^*}{S_0} \times \Delta S\)
\[AFN = \frac{A^* - L^*}{S_0} \times \Delta S - PM \times RR \times S_1 + \text{req. fixed asset}\] \[PEFR = \frac{A^* - L^*}{S_0} - PM \times RR \times \frac{1+g}{g} = \frac{AFN}{\Delta S}\] \(S_1 = S_0(1+g)\), \(RR = 1 - DPR\), DPR = Div / NI, PM = NI / Sales

Full capacity adjustment

Full capacity sales = Actual sales / % capacity
Target FA/sales = Fixed assets / Full capacity sales
Required fixed assets = Target ratio × Projected sales

Regression (y = a + bx)

\(b = \frac{N\sum xy - \sum x \sum y}{N\sum x^2 - (\sum x)^2}\) ; \(a = \frac{\sum y - b \sum x}{N}\)
