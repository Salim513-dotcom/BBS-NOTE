üéØQualitative Research

What is Qualitative Research?

Research connected to subjective phenomena that explores and interprets perceptions, opinions, behaviors, motivation, culture, and attitudes of people.

Key Point: Deals with human and social behaviors, mentality, and intuition - not just data and facts.

‚≠êFeatures of Qualitative Research

1Ô∏è‚É£ Studying Real-World Conditions

Studies people's lives under real-world conditions. Captures deep internal thoughts beyond simple yes/no responses. Extracts real perspectives that people don't normally express.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Representing Views & Perspectives

Different people have different views. Involves real events not just majority data. Captures perspectives even from minorities.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Covering Contextual Conditions

Events happen under certain conditions. Watches conditions properly and explores reality, not just results.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Contributing Insights

Based on reality of events. Watches both existing and emerging concepts in social sectors. Explains insights of people.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Multiple Sources of Evidence

Uses different data sources and multidimensional views. Studies real life through depth observations and interviews.

üî¨Methods of Qualitative Research

1Ô∏è‚É£ Case Study

üìÅ

Deals with a single case rather than a sample or population. Solves particular problem of specific organization. Results cannot be generalized to others.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Grounded Theory

üèóÔ∏è

Developed by Glaser & Strauss (1967). Complex interactive process with generative questions. Links gathered data with theoretical concepts. Elements: concepts, categories, propositions.

3Ô∏è‚É£ Ethnographic Study

üåç

Studies intact cultural group in natural setting over specific period. Groups share common social experience, location, or characteristics. Real-life study of particular ethnic groups.

4Ô∏è‚É£ Phenomenological Study

üí´

Study of human experiences and structures within which humans experience the world. Focuses on subjective experiences and interpretations. Long-time study of particular groups.

5Ô∏è‚É£ Participatory Research

ü§ù

Based on participatory management theory. Related people made participants in every action. Results distributed across organization simultaneously.

6Ô∏è‚É£ Field Research

üéí

Reaches field to see reality and actual conditions. Observes activities, physical conditions of locality. Creates notes, reports, and collects opinions.
