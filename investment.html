Chapter 1 · The investment environment

Key definitions

  • Investment: Commitment of current resources in expectation of greater future resources.
  • Real Assets: Assets used to purchase goods and services.
  • Financial Assets: Claim on income and other assets.
  • Saving: Excess of income over expenditure.
  • Speculation: Expectation of windfall gain in very short period with excessive risk.
  • Gambling: A game to satisfy personal desire.

Types of investments

  • Securities or properties
  • Direct or indirect investments
  • Debt, equity or derivative securities
  • Low or high risk investments
  • Short or long term investments
  • Domestic or foreign investments

Investors & process

  • Individual investors / Institutional investors
  • Investment process: security analysis → construct portfolio → revision → evaluate performance
  • Vehicles: short‑term, common stock, fixed income, mutual funds, derivatives

Steps in investing

  1. Meeting investment prerequisites
  2. Establishing investment goals
  3. Adopting an investment plan
  4. Evaluating investment vehicles
  5. Selecting suitable investments
  6. Constructing a portfolio
  7. Managing the portfolio

Chapter 2 · Securities market

Long (buy) position

Cash basis return: \[\frac{P_1 - P_0 + D_1}{P_0}\]
Margin basis return: \[\frac{P_1 - P_0 + D_1 - I}{P_0 \times IM}\]
\(I = P_0 \times N \times (1-IM) \times k\) (interest)
InitialAfter
Asset: \(N\cdot P_0\)\(N\cdot P_1\)
Debt: \(N\cdot P_0(1-IM)\)\(N\cdot P_0(1-IM)\)
Equity: \(N\cdot P_0\cdot IM\)\(N\cdot P_1 - N\cdot P_0(1-IM)\)
Margin call (long): Actual margin \(AM = \frac{\text{Equity}}{\text{Asset}} = \frac{\text{Asset} - \text{Debt}}{\text{Asset}}\)
If \(AM < MM\) (maintenance margin), call.
Minimum collateral: \(MC = \frac{\text{Debt}}{1-MM}\)
Trigger price: \(TP = \frac{1-IM}{1-MM} \times P_0\)
If actual price \(< TP\), call.

Short (sell) position

Cash basis return: \[\frac{P_0 - P_1 - D_1}{P_0}\]
Margin basis return: \[\frac{P_0 - P_1 - D_1 + I}{P_0 \times IM}\]
\(I = P_0 \times N \times IM \times k\)
InitialAfter
Asset: \(N\cdot P_0(1+IM)\)\(N\cdot P_0(1+IM)\)
Debt: \(N\cdot P_0\)\(N\cdot P_1\)
Equity: \(N\cdot P_0\cdot IM\)\(N\cdot P_0(1+IM) - N\cdot P_1\)
Margin call (short): \(AM = \frac{\text{Equity}}{\text{Debt}} = \frac{\text{Asset} - \text{Debt}}{\text{Debt}}\)
If \(AM < MM\), call.
Minimum collateral: \(MC = \frac{\text{Asset}}{1+MM}\)
Trigger price: \(TP = \frac{1+IM}{1+MM} \times P_0\)
If actual price \(> TP\), call.

Chapter 3 · Investment information & indices

Price index numbers

\[\text{PWI} = \frac{\sum \text{current prices}}{\sum \text{base prices}} \times \text{base value}\] \[\text{Revised divisor} = \text{Previous divisor} \times \frac{\sum \text{new prices}}{\sum \text{old prices}}\]

Value‑weighted index

\[VWI = \frac{\sum (\text{current price} \times \text{shares})}{\sum (\text{base price} \times \text{shares})} \times 100\]

Equally‑weighted index

\[EWI = \frac{\sum \text{holding period returns}}{\text{number of stocks}} \times 100\]

Rate of return on indices

\[R_{PWI} = \frac{PWI_1 - PWI_0}{PWI_0} \times 100\] \[R_{VWI} = \frac{VWI_1 - VWI_0}{VWI_0} \times 100\] \[R_{EWI} = \frac{EWI_1 - EWI_0}{EWI_0} \times 100\]

Chapter 6 · Common stock analysis & valuation

Per share & yield ratios

BVPS = \(\frac{\text{Total assets} - \text{Debt} - \text{Preferred stock}}{\text{Number of common shares}}\)
EPS = \(\frac{\text{NPAT} - \text{Preferred dividend}}{\text{Common shares}}\)
Dividend yield (common) = \(\frac{DPS}{MVPS}\)
Dividend yield (preferred) = \(\frac{DPS_{\text{PS}}}{MVPS_{\text{PS}}}\)
Dividend payout = \(\frac{DPS}{EPS}\)
P/E ratio = \(\frac{MVPS}{EPS}\)
P/E growth = \(\frac{\text{PE ratio}}{\text{earnings growth rate}}\)
If PE growth = 1 → correctly priced
>1 → overvalued; <1 → undervalued

Financial ratios (liquidity, leverage, turnover)

  • Current ratio = CA / CL
  • Quick ratio = (CA - inventory - prepaid) / CL
  • Working capital = CA - CL
  • Cash ratio = (cash + marketable) / CL
  • Debt ratio = Debt / Assets
  • Debt/Equity = Debt / Equity
  • Equity multiplier = Assets / Equity
  • Times interest earned = EBIT / Interest
  • Fixed asset turnover = Sales / Fixed assets
  • Total asset turnover = Sales / Total assets
  • Inventory turnover = COGS / Inventory or Sales / Inventory
  • Receivables turnover = Sales / Receivables or Credit sales / Receivables
  • Capital employed turnover = Sales / Capital employed

Profitability & DuPont

ROA = Net income / Total assets
ROE = Net income / Equity
Net profit margin = Net income / Sales
ROA = PM × ATOR
ROE = PM × ATOR × EM

Dividend discount models

Zero growth: \(P_0 = \frac{D}{k}\)
Constant growth: \(P_0 = \frac{D_1}{k - g}\)
Multi‑stage: \(P_0 = \frac{D_1}{(1+k)} + \frac{D_2}{(1+k)^2} + \frac{D_3}{(1+k)^3} + \frac{P_3}{(1+k)^3}\), with \(P_3 = \frac{D_4}{k - g}\)

Price multiple approaches

P/E approach: \(P_0 = EPS \times PE\)
Price to cash flow: \(P_0 = CFPS \times P/CF\)
Price to book: \(P_0 = BVPS \times P/BV\)
Price to sales: \(P_0 = SPS \times P/S\)

Fixed income securities

Yield measures

Bank discount yield = \(\frac{FV - P_0}{FV} \times \frac{360}{n}\)
Annual equivalent yield = \(\frac{FV - P_0}{P_0} \times \frac{365}{n}\)
HPR = \(\frac{FV - P_0}{P_0}\)
Effective annual yield = \((1 + HPR)^m - 1\)
Annual HPR = \(\frac{HPR}{t} \times 360\)

Municipal & corporate

Taxable equivalent rate (muni) = \(\frac{\text{Muni yield}}{1 - \text{tax rate}}\)
After‑tax yield (corp) = Corp yield \(\times (1 - \text{tax rate})\)

Bond pricing

\(V_0 = I \times \frac{1 - (1+k)^{-n}}{k} + M \times (1+k)^{-n}\)
Zero coupon: \(V_0 = M \times (1+k)^{-n}\)
Perpetual: \(V_0 = \frac{I}{k}\)
Callable: \(V_0 = I \times \frac{1 - (1+k)^{-n_c}}{k} + CP \times (1+k)^{-n_c}\)

Yield to maturity

Approx YTM = \(\frac{I + \frac{M - V_0}{n}}{\frac{M + 2V_0}{3}}\) Interpolation: \(LR + \frac{PV_{LR} - PV}{PV_{LR} - PV_{HR}} \times (HR - LR)\)
Zero coupon YTM = \(\left(\frac{FV}{P_0}\right)^{1/n} - 1\)
Perpetual YTM = \(\frac{I}{V_0}\)

Duration

Duration (Macaulay) = \(\frac{\sum PV(t) \times t}{\sum PV}\)
Modified duration = \(\frac{\text{Duration}}{1 + y}\)
%Δ price ≈ \(-\text{Modified duration} \times \Delta i\)

Convertible securities

Conversion ratio (CR) = \(\frac{\text{Face value of convertible}}{\text{Conversion price}}\)
Conversion price (CP) = \(\frac{\text{Face value}}{CR}\)
Initial conversion premium = \(\frac{CP - P_0}{P_0}\)
Conversion parity = \(\frac{\text{Market value of convertible}}{CR}\)
Conversion value (Ct) = \(CR \times P_0\)
Straight bond value (Bt) = \(I \times PVIFA(k_d, n) + M \times PVIF(k_d, n)\)
Min value = \(\max(C_t, B_t)\)
Conversion premium (subsequent) = \(\frac{\text{Market value} - \max(B_t,C_t)}{\max(B_t,C_t)}\)
Payback period = \(\frac{\text{Conversion premium}}{\text{Annual interest} - \text{Annual dividend}}\)

Mutual funds

Load & NAV

Purchase commission: \(P_0 \times (1 + c\%)\)
Sales commission: \(S_0 \times (1 - c\%)\)
Premium = \(NAV \times (1 + \text{premium%})\)
Discount = \(NAV \times (1 - \text{discount%})\)
Load fee = \(\frac{NAV}{1 - \text{load%}}\)

Return & expense

Open‑end (no load): \(\frac{NAV_1 - NAV_0}{NAV_0}\)
Open‑end (load): \(\frac{NAV_1 - OP}{OP}\)
Close‑end: \(\frac{P_1 - P_0}{P_0}\)
Expense ratio = \(\frac{\text{Expenses}}{\text{Beginning NAV}}\)
Portfolio turnover = \(\frac{\min(\text{buy, sell})}{\text{Average NAV}}\)

Portfolio performance measures

HPR = \(\frac{V_1 - V_0 + C_1}{V_0}\)
After‑tax HPR = \(HPR \times (1 - t)\)
HPR on portfolio = \(\frac{RCG + URCG + C_1}{E_0 + N_f \times \frac{12 - I_p}{12} - W_f \times \frac{12 - W_p}{12}}\)
Sharpe (portfolio) \(S_p = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}\)
Treynor \(T_p = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\beta_p}\)
Jensen \(\alpha_p = R_p - [R_f + (R_m - R_f)\beta_p]\)
Sharpe (market) \(S_m = \frac{R_m - R_f}{\sigma_m}\)
Treynor (market) \(T_m = \frac{R_m - R_f}{\beta_m}\)
Jensen (market) \(\alpha_m = R_m - [R_f + (R_m - R_f)\beta_m]\)

Options

Value of call option = \(\max[(P_0 - X), 0]\)
Value of put option = \(\max[(X - P_0), 0]\)
Rate of return = \(\frac{\text{Profit}}{\text{Premium}} = \frac{\text{Value} - \text{Premium}}{\text{Premium}}\)

Margin call example (long & short)

Long: \(P_0 = 1000, P_1 = 1200, IM=40\%, MM=35\%\)

Return cash = \(\frac{1200-1000}{1000}=20\%\)
Return margin = \(\frac{1200-1000 - I}{1000\times0.4}\) with \(I = 1000\times0.6\times k\)
Balance sheet at \(P_1=1200\): Equity=600, Debt=600, Asset=1200
AM = 600/1200=50% >35% → no call
MC = 600/(1-0.35)=923 < 1200 → no call
TP = \(\frac{1-0.4}{1-0.35}\times1000=923\) → 1200 > TP → no call
At \(P_1=800\): Equity=200, Asset=800, AM=25% <35% → call
MC = 600/0.65=923 >800 → call
TP=923 >800 → call

Short: \(P_0=1000, IM=40\%, MM=35\%\)

Initial asset = 1400, debt=1000, equity=400.
At \(P_1=1200\): asset=1400, debt=1200, equity=200
AM = 200/1200=16.7% <35% → call
MC = 1400/(1+0.35)=1037 < 1200 → call
TP = \(\frac{1+0.4}{1+0.35}\times1000=1037\) → 1200 > TP → call
At \(P_1=800\): equity=600, debt=800, AM=75% >35% → no call
MC=1037 >800 → no call, TP=1037 >800 → no call
