üìùResearch Proposal

A research proposal is a presentation of the idea in which the report or thesis is made. It is a road map of making report or thesis from beginning to end including the location and ideas. It is a research plan showing the path of data collection, analysis and interpretation.


It is a written document explaining what is done, how it is done, and what will be obtained from the study.

üèóÔ∏èStructure of Research Proposal

Structure of the research depends on the nature of the research. Each and every proposal should follow a certain authentic design. According to the university it may differ and some structures may be pre-defined by any organization.

Title

Should be neither too short nor long but appropriate for the subject matter. Should not contain unnecessary words but sensible to reflect the gist.

Example: Brand Loyalty of the Detergent Users in Nepal
Example: Effectiveness of VAT in Nepal

Background of the Study

Provides general information about the research title in brief. May give relevant literature, reference material, creation of interest in topic and rational of the proposal study.

Statement of the Problem

Main focus of the study to solve a problem or resolution of the issue. Write clearly about the problem, show proper relationship between study and variable, and ensure problem is solvable.

Theoretical Framework

Helps to develop background of the study. Shows relationship between study and variables. Should inform about: summary of literature, identification of variables, theory's presentation.

Statement of the Hypothesis

A declarative statement showing relationship between variables. Must be declarative, measureable, and without ambiguity (vagueness).

Definition of Terminologies

All technical terms are properly defined for understanding of all readers.

Significance of the Study

Shows where the study is used, how it contributes, its relevance and benefits. Must create prospective to look at problem, show relationship to larger issues, and demonstrate benefits after completion.

Limitation of the Study

Declares the area which is not attempted by researcher due to different reasons (time, cost, behavior of people, secondary data, quality constraints).

Research Methodology

Contains: Research Design, Population and Sample, Instruments to be used, Data Collection Strategies, Analysis of the Data.

References

List of literatures used in proposal writing, arranged in alphabetical order. Must follow APA format (American Psychology Association).

üìëContent of Research Report

Research reports may not be similar everywhere. Formats, structure and subject matters vary from universities. Generally, reports contain three main parts:

1

Preliminary Part

Title Page, Recommendation Sheet, Viva-Voice Sheet, Acknowledgement, Executive Summary, Table of Content, List of Tables and Figures

2

Body of Report

Introduction, Review of Literature, Research Methodology, Data Presentation and Analysis, Summary and Conclusion

3

Supplementary Part

References & Bibliography, Appendix

üìãA. Preliminary Part

1. Title Page

The first and outermost page including:

  • Title of the Research/Thesis
  • Name of the Researcher
  • Name of the institution whom the report is submitted to
  • Degree for which the Report is submitted
  • Name of the place submitted
  • Date of submitting

2. Recommendation Sheet

Contains signatures from Supervisor, Head of Research Department, and Campus Chief/HOD certifying the thesis meets requirements.

3. Viva-Voice Sheet

Contains findings from viva-voice examination with signatures from Research Department Head, Thesis Supervisor, and External Expert.

4. Acknowledgement

Summary of procedure, purpose, scope, methodologies. Thanks to those directly or indirectly involved in making the report.

5. Executive Summary/Synopsis

Information about the research study, overview, problem solved in investigation, data collection methods, sample selected, findings and suggestions. Length around two pages.

6. Table of Contents

Provides information about placement of various heads and subheads with page numbers. Main heads in capital letters, small heads in lowercase.

7. List of Tables and Figures

Every table and figure with their numbers and page numbers listed separately.

üìñB. Body of the Report (Main Part)

Chapter 1: Introduction

First unit containing background of topic and situational factors. Includes:

  • Statement of the problem
  • Objective of the study
  • Theoretical framework
  • Variables
  • Research hypothesis
  • Definition of the concept
  • Limitations of the study
  • Organization of the study

Chapter 2: Review of Literature

Gives relevancy between problem and theory. Materials managed serially under depth of knowledge. Findings of earlier researchers taken. Concluding remarks should show research gap and relevance.

Chapter 3: Research Methodology

Describes how research is done in reality. Includes:

  • Research design
  • Population and sample
  • Sampling procedure
  • Data gathering procedures
  • Variables and measures
  • Data processing procedures
  • Statistical procedures
  • Pilot study
  • Limitations of methodology

Chapter 4: Data Presentation and Analysis

Presents data, tables, charts, diagrams systematically:

  • Presentation of the data
  • Descriptive analysis
  • Correlation analysis
  • Inferential analysis

Chapter 5: Summary, Conclusion and Recommendation

Summary: Shows importance of hypothesis to develop theory and solution. Brief review of entire work.

Conclusion: Concludes major findings and describes implications for decision making. Shows difference between old and new improvements.

Recommendation: Gives suggestions (not in all reports, mainly for non-academic research). Describes main contribution and facilitates further investigation.

üìöC. Supplementary Section

References/Bibliography

List of materials' sources used to make the report. May include books, articles, reports, journals, pictures, or net-based materials. Must follow APA format (American Psychology Association).

Appendix

Contains charts, figures, data, and long questionnaires not fit in main body. Kept for further justification. If many, labeled as Appendix "A", "B", etc. Page numbers usually in Roman numerals (i, ii, iii... or I, II, III...).
