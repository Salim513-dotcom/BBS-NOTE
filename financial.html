10 types of risk (with definitions)

  • 1 Credit risk – borrowers fail to repay interest and principal
  • 2 Liquidity risk – sudden unexpected cash outflow forces asset fire sales
    • Asset side: commitment can’t be honored due to lack of funds
    • Liability side: depositors’ demand cannot be met immediately
  • 3 Interest rate risk – volatile rates & mismatch in asset/liability maturities
  • 4 Market risk – from trading securities
  • 5 Off balance sheet risk – assets not appearing on balance sheet
  • 6 Foreign exchange risk – exchange rate changes affect value of foreign assets/liabilities
  • 7 Sovereign risk – repayments from foreign borrowers interrupted by foreign government
  • 8 Technology risk – tech investments fail to produce anticipated benefits due to high obsolescence
  • 9 Operational risk – losses from inadequate internal processes, people, systems or external events
  • 10 Insolvency risk – not enough capital to offset sudden decline in asset value relative to liabilities

Chapter 2 · Determinants of Interest Rate

Treasury & corporate formulas

Treasury Bill = K* + IPn
Treasury Bond = K* + IPn + MRPRn
Corporate Bond = K* + IPn + MRPRn + LRPRn + DRPRn

Algebraic & geometric methods

IP2 = (I1+I2)/2
K1 = K* + IP1
K2 = K* + IP2
K3 = K* + IP3
0K1 = K* + I1
1K2 = K* + I2
2K3 = K* + I3
tKn = 〖((1+Kn)^n/(1+Kt)^t )〗^(1/(n−t))-1
Pure expectation theory: No MRPR, LRPR, DRPR in Kn or Kt.

Points to remember (pages 2-3)

  • 1 year bonds: 1 year from now = 1K2, during 1st year = 0K1, after 1 year = 0K1
  • Upward sloping yield curve conditions: a) inflationary trend expected to increase → long term rates higher; b) maturity risk premium higher on long term securities
  • Economy in recovery → inflation increases
  • One year interest rate expected in year 3 is higher than that of 3 year because of expected increase in future inflation
  • X = Maturity (Years), Y = Yield Rate
  • Longer term bonds exposed to maturity risk due to higher interest rate risk
  • Yield on 3 year securities larger than 2 year securities because inflation rate in 3rd year is higher than inflation premium over 3
  • Default risk: investors may not receive principal & interest
  • Corporate bonds exposed to default risk due to economic uncertainty
  • Pure expectation theory assumes MRPR, DRPR, LRP = 0%

Tools of monetary policy

  • Open market operations: Buy → increase reserve; Sell → decrease reserve
  • Discount loan/discount rates: Increase → increase reserve; Decrease → decrease reserve
  • Reserve requirement: Increase → increase reserve, decrease demand deposit; Decrease → decrease reserve, increase demand deposit

Formulas & multipliers

Required Reserve = Demand Deposit × REQ
Demand Deposit = Required Reserve / REQ
Deposit Multiplier = 1 / REQ
Money Multiplier = (1 + C/D) / (REQ + ER/D + C/D)   (e.g., Cash reserve ratio=10, C/D=10/90)
Change in Deposit = (1 / (REQn + C)) × ΔR × RCR
Change in Deposit = New Deposit – Old Deposit (repeated)

Balance sheet changes (for answers)

  • Change in Reserve = D × (REQ – REQn)
  • Change in Deposit = (1 / (REQn + C)) × ΔR × RCR
  • Reserve: Total = New Deposit × REQn; Cash = ΔR × (1 – RCR); Reserve = Total – Cash
  • Loan = Deposit – Reserve – Cash
  • Discount loan increases reserve and liabilities of Commercial Bank
  • 4 impacts: Central Bank Asset (Discount loan ↑), Central Bank Liabilities (Reserve ↑), Commercial Bank Asset (Reserve ↑), Commercial Bank Liabilities (Discount Loan ↑)

Key ratios (page 6,8,9)

Income ratio = (Interest + Non-Interest) / Total Operating Income
Expense ratio = (Interest + Non-Interest + Provision for loan loss + Tax) / Asset
Spread = (Interest Income / Earnings Assets) – (Interest Expense / Earnings Assets) ; Earnings Assets = Investment securities + Net loan and leases
Overhead Efficiency = Non-Interest Income / Non-Interest Expenses
Core Capital Ratio = (Core Capital / Total RWA) × 100% (min 6%)
Capital Adequacy Ratio = (Total Capital / Total RWA) × 100% (min 8.5%)
Leverage Ratio = Core Capital / Total RWA
Total RWA = Credit RWA + Market RWA + Operational RWA + Supervisory Adjustment
Credit RWA = RWA × Asset ; Operational RWA = Capital Charge × 11 (α=15) ; Market RWA = Capital Charge × 11, Capital Charge = Total Open Position × 5%, Total Open Position = OP(foreign) × OP(Domestic) ; Supervisory Adjustment = 3% of Total RWA excluding Supervisory Adj.

Portfolio quality ratios (page 8)

  • Portfolio at risk ratio = (overdue amount) / Gross loan portfolio
  • Written off / Loan loss ratio = Loan written off in period / Average loan portfolio ( >2% indicative of delinquency)
  • Loan loss reserve ratio = Impairment allowance for loan loss / Gross loan portfolio
  • Risk Coverage ratio = Impairment allowance for loan loss / Amount of overdue loan
  • Non-performing loan ratio = NPL / Gross loan portfolio
  • Delinquent borrower rate = Number of delinquent borrowers / Total active borrowers

Profitability & sustainability (page 8-9)

  • Return on assets = Net Income / Average Total Assets
  • Return on equity = Net Income / Average Equity
  • Portfolio Yield = (Interest, fee and commission income in cash) / Average gross loan portfolio
  • Cost of funds = (Interest, fee and commission income) / (Deposit and borrowings)
  • Spread Ratio = (Interest income / Earnings assets) – (Interest expense / Liabilities) [from page 8]
  • Operational self-sufficiency = Operating Income / (Operating and financing cost + Impairment charge)
  • Operational self-sufficiency (with cost of capital) = Operating Income / (Operating and financing cost + Impairment charge + Cost of capital) ; Cost of capital = Adjustment of Equity
  • Subsidy dependence Index = Total annual subsidies received / Average annual interest income

Leverage, productivity & efficiency (page 9)

  • Debt/Equity ratio = Debt / Equity
  • Leverage ratio = Equity / Asset (or as given)
  • Capital Adequacy Ratio = Total capital fund / Total RWA
  • Number of active loans per staff = Total active borrowers / Total active staff
  • Number of active clients per staff = Total active clients / Total active staff
  • Cost per loan = Total operating cost / Total number of borrowers
  • Cost per active client = Total operating cost / Total number of loans
  • Average loan outstanding = Gross loan portfolio / Total borrowers
  • Average loan disbursed = Value of loans disbursed during period / Number of loans disbursed
  • Operating cost ratio = Total operating cost / Average value of loan portfolio (use average when comparing with income statement; use gross when comparing with balance sheet)

Solvency & other (page 10)

Solvency ratio = Net total asset / (Deposit and share capital)
Bank Borrowing = External Capital
Institutional capital = Reserve fund + Other fund
Net Institutional capital = Reserve fund + Other fund – Prov > 1m
Net liquidity = Short term investment + Cash + Bank – Current liability

Chapter 5 · Insurance company ratios

All insurance ratios

  • Loss ratio = Loss disbursed / Premium earned
    Loss disbursed = Net claim paid + Increase in claim reserve
  • Expense ratio = Expenses / Premium earned
  • Combined ratio = Loss ratio + Expense ratio
  • Dividend ratio = Dividend to shareholders / Premium earned
  • Combined ratio after dividend = LR + ER + DR
  • Investment Yield = Investment income / Premium earned
  • Operating Ratio = Combined ratio after dividend – Investment yield
  • Overall profitability = 100% – Operating ratio
  • Premium growth rate = (Current year – Last year) / Last year
  • Risk retention ratio = Net insurance premium / Gross insurance premium
  • Gross earning ratio = Net profit / Gross insurance premium
  • Operating leverage = Net worth / Available solvency margin amount
  • Solvency margin = Available solvency margin amount / Required solvency margin amount ; Available = Total RWA – RWL
  • Yield on Investment = Income from Investment and Loan / Total Investment and Loan
  • Operating expenses ratio = Operating expenses / Net insurance premium
  • Premium to asset ratio = Gross insurance premium / Total assets

Chapter 6 · Mutual funds

Load, discount, premium

  • Commission on purchase = Po × (1 + c%)
  • Commission on sales = So × (1 – c%)
  • Premium = NAV × (1 + Premium%)
  • Discount = NAV × (1 – Discount%)
  • Load fee = NAV / (1 – Load fee)

Return calculations

  • Open-ended (no load): (NAV₁ – NAV₀) / NAV₀
  • Open-ended (with load): (NAV₁ – OP) / OP
  • Close-ended: (P₁ – P₀) / P₀
  • Expense ratio = Expenses / Beginning NAV
  • Portfolio turnover ratio = Min(buying, selling) / Average NAV

Chapter 7 · Pension funds

Annual retirement benefits

  • Years of service × Benefit per year of service
  • Years of service × Average salary × Benefit payout ratio
  • Years of service × Average salary of last some years × Benefit payout ratio

Accumulation formulas

One year return = (Value at year end – Net contribution) / Net contribution
Monthly pension = (Value of service + Amount of last salary) / 50
Annual retirement income = Funds available for retirement × Vesting ratio × Annuity rate
